# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Modern Malaysian couples aged 20-40 planning their wedding. They're digitally literate, WhatsApp-native, and cost-aware. They share invitations via link, expect mobile-first experiences, and are annoyed by the expense and rigidity of traditional printed cards. Both partners may co-manage the invitation. Guests range from tech-savvy to elderly relatives who just need a tap-friendly page.

## Product Purpose

Jemputan Digital (jemputandigital.my) is a self-serve SaaS platform for creating, customizing, and sharing digital wedding invitations. Couples create a slug-based public URL, manage RSVPs, collect wishes and wishlists, and track guest analytics — all for a one-time fee of RM39. No monthly subscription. Create in 5 minutes, share via WhatsApp.

Success looks like: a couple sets up their invitation before the day is over, shares it confidently, and doesn't need to touch a spreadsheet again.

## Brand Personality

Modern, practical, Malay-first.

Voice: conversational and warm, but never cutesy. Speaks like a savvy friend who happens to know tech. Uses Malay as the default, switches to English for technical clarity. Respects the cultural weight of a wedding without being stuffy about it. Empowers couples to feel in control, not overwhelmed.

Emotional goal: relief. The couple should feel like someone sorted this out for them.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS** (cream backgrounds, Inter font, blue CTAs, hero metrics): Jemputan Digital is not a B2B tool. It should not look like a project management app or a startup landing page template.
- **Tacky e-wedding portals**: no glitter, no clipart roses, no Comic Sans, no garish gradients. The old-generation Malaysian wedding website aesthetic is the thing this product replaces.
- **Cold/clinical minimalism**: Scandinavian whitespace with zero warmth is wrong for weddings. This is a celebratory product. It should feel alive.

## Design Principles

1. **Culturally grounded, not stereotypically Malaysian**: The design should feel native to the Malaysian context without falling back on batik motifs or gold filigree. Modern interpretation of warmth and celebration.
2. **Relief, not excitement**: The primary emotion is relief — this was easier than expected. Not hype, not overwhelm. Clean flows, clear actions, no noise.
3. **Celebration lives in the product, not the chrome**: The invitation itself is where beauty belongs. The dashboard and builder chrome should be restrained so the couple's content shines.
4. **Mobile is real**: Guests open invitations on their phone in a WhatsApp chat. Every interaction must work perfectly at 390px. This is not a responsive afterthought.
5. **Honest about price**: RM39 one-time is a genuine differentiator. The pricing story should be stated plainly, not buried or over-decorated.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum. High contrast for small text, especially on the public guest invitation page where elderly guests are a real use case.
- Tap targets minimum 44px. The guest RSVP flow must be operable by someone unfamiliar with smartphones.
- No reliance on color alone for state (RSVP status, form errors).
- Malay as default language; English available. No assumption of English literacy.
